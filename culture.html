Festivals

Bastar Dussehra is a unique and vibrant festival celebrated in the Bastar region of Chhattisgarh,
India. Unlike the typical Dussehra that marks Lord Rama's victory over Ravana, Bastar Dussehra is
dedicated to the local deity, Danteshwari Mai, and involves rituals, traditions, and customs that
span 75 days. This festival reflects the rich tribal culture of the region and includes activities such
as the installation of the sacred pillar, processions, traditional dances, and the grand
Rath Yatra (chariot procession). Bastar Dussehra is a time when the entire community comes together to
celebrate their heritage, spirituality, and the deep connection with their land and deities.

Art and Handicrafts

(1.) - Dhokra Art: Intricate metal sculptures made using the lost-wax casting technique, featuring tribal
deities, animals, and human figures

(2.) - Wooden Crafts: Carved wooden items such as masks, sculptures, and panels depicting tribal life
and mythological themes.

(3.) - Wrought Iron Crafts: Rustic iron items like lamps, wall hangings, and sculptures, often showcasing tribal
folklore and daily life.

(4.) -Tumba Art (Coconut Craft): Decorative objects made from dried gourd shells, intricately carved and sometimes painted.

(5.)-Bamboo Crafts: Durable and intricately woven items like baskets, furniture, and musical instruments.
