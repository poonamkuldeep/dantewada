Danteshwari Temple, located in Dantewada, Chhattisgarh, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to
the goddess Danteshwari, a form of Durga. This ancient temple, dating back over a thousand years,
is a prominent pilgrimage site for devotees. It is famed for its architectural elegance and
sacred ambiance, surrounded by lush forests and hills. The temple is not only a spiritual center but also
a cultural landmark, hosting the grand annual Bastar Dussehra festival, which attracts thousands of pilgrims and tourists. The Danteshwari Temple stands as a symbol of faith, tradition,
and the rich heritage of the Bastar region.

Dholkal is a historic hill located in Dantewada, Chhattisgarh, known for its ancient Dholkal Ganesh Temple.
Perched on a hilltop, this temple features a 1,000-year-old statue of Lord Ganesha, carved from
a single piece of stone. The site is renowned for its panoramic views and serene atmosphere,
making it a popular pilgrimage and tourist destination. Dholkal's historical and spiritual significance,
combined with its scenic beauty, makes it a treasured gem in the Bastar region.

Barsur, located in the Dantewada district of Chhattisgarh, is an ancient town renowned for its rich historical
and cultural heritage. Once the capital of the Nagavanshi dynasty, Barsur is famous for
its numerous temples and archaeological ruins dating back to the 10th and 11th centuries.
Key attractions include the twin Ganesha statues, Mama-Bhanja temple, and Battisa temple, each
showcasing intricate stone carvings and architectural brilliance. Barsur's historical significance
and its collection of ancient temples make it a captivating destination for history
enthusiasts and spiritual seekers.
